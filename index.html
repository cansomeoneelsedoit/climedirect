Clever Clime — Framework Reference

Multi-sector equity valuation framework · Clime Investment Management · March 2026 · Confidential

Abstract: Clever Clime extends the SIV equity multiplier into a sector-adaptive, multi-method system. It retains the equity multiplier as the primary engine for industrials and financials, introduces sector-specific methodologies where the multiplier breaks down, adds an earnings quality gate, and integrates a quality-momentum overlay to translate static IV signals into dynamic investment decisions.
5 Valuation Tiers
1
Tier 1 — SIV Core
Consumer Discretionary · Consumer Staples · Health Care (established) · Industrials · Banks · Insurance · Diversified Financials · IT (profitable)
Equity Multiplier: IV = Equity/Share × [(NROE/RR × PR) + (NROE²/RR² × [1−PR])]
2
Tier 2 — AEG Model
Software & Services · Technology Hardware (asset-light) · Professional Services · Health Care Technology
AEG: IV = EPS(FY+1) / (WACC−g_LT) × [1 + (g_ST−g_LT)/WACC]
3
Tier 3 — EV/EBIT
Energy · Materials · Utilities · Communications (infra-heavy) · Transportation Infrastructure
EV/EBIT: IV = (EBIT(FY+1) × Target Multiple − Net Debt) / Shares
4
Tier 4 — FFO/AFFO
Equity REITs · Diversified Real Estate · Infrastructure Funds
P/AFFO: IV = AFFO per Unit × Target P/AFFO Multiple
5
Tier 5 — Revenue Multiple
Pre-profit technology · Biotech · Early-stage healthcare · Companies with negative equity
EV/NTM Revenue vs peer group median. Directional signals only — no absolute IV produced. Position sizing governed by qualitative assessment and stop-loss discipline.

Earnings Quality Score (EQS)
Composite of 4 metrics, each 0–25 pts, max 100. Score ≥70 required to act on IV signal. 50–70 triggers review flag. <50 suspends the IV signal.
25
Earnings Stability
CV of trailing 5-year normalised EPS. Low coefficient of variation = more predictable earnings base.
25
Cash Conversion
Operating CF / NPAT over 3 years. Values below 0.7 indicate accrual-heavy earnings quality concern.
25
Balance Sheet Integrity
Net debt / EBITDA trend. Deteriorating leverage concurrent with growing earnings = quality warning.
25
Consensus Revision Trend
Direction of analyst EPS revisions over 90 days. Sustained downward revisions discount IV reliability.

How It Works

STRETD simulates holding a stock over the nominated period, reinvesting each year's after-tax dividend by purchasing more shares at the interpolated price.

Each year: EPS grows at your forecast rate → dividend paid (EPS × payout) → tax deducted → net dividend purchases more shares at interpolated price.

At exit: all shares sold at EPS(n) × exit P/E. Capital gain tax on original share only. Return = CAGR of total proceeds vs entry price.

2
The spreadsheet works out fair value automatically
Once you paste the data in, the Valuation sheet does the maths. Here is what happens in plain English:
📐
How well does it use capital?
Earnings divided by book value. A high number means the business is efficient.
🇦🇺
Franking credit uplift
AU tax credits make fully-franked dividends worth more. Added automatically.
✖️
A single fair value multiplier
Income value + growth value combined into one number, multiplied by book value.
🏷️
The signal
>15% below fair value = Buy. Within 15% = Hold. >15% above = Sell.
3
Check earnings quality before buying
A cheap stock is only interesting if the profits are real. The EQS sheet scores each company on 4 questions. Score 70+ to act.
📊
Are earnings stable?
Consistent profits over 5 years. Volatile = risky.
💵
Does profit turn into cash?
Paper profit that doesn't become cash is a warning sign.
⚖️
Is debt under control?
Growing profits while piling on debt is a red flag.
📈
Are analysts upgrading?
If forecasts are being cut, wait. Net upgrades in FactSet via CESTIREV_EPS.
70–100
Act on signal
50–69

<50
Don't buy
4
Check price momentum — timing matters
A stock can be cheap for a long time. We use one simple timing check: is it higher than 12 months ago?
Price up vs 12 months ago
Market starting to see the value → Active Buy. Full position.
Price down vs 12 months ago
Still cheap but falling → Accumulate. Half position, add later.
FactSet code: P_PRICE_CHG(-252,-21,ANN) — 12-month return excluding the most recent month
